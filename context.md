# Story Overview

**"Boston Bleeds Again"** (working title) is a **psychological thriller** wrapped around a story of **family reconciliation**.

## The Core Story

Karisa Yeandel returns to Boston after five years away, hoping to rebuild her late father's beloved bookstore and quietly restart her life in the Beacon Hill apartment she once fled. What begins as a story about grief, homecoming, and reopening a shuttered family business gradually reveals itself to be something much darker.

## The Two Intertwined Threads

**1. The Sisters' Reunion** The three estranged Yeandel sisters — cold, controlled Aesmine; warm, exiled Millenka; and quiet, guilt-ridden Karisa — are pulled back into each other's orbit for the first time in years. Old wounds resurface: Millenka's elopement with David, Aesmine's fury at being "abandoned" during their father's death, Karisa's silent disappearance five years ago. Their tentative, imperfect reconciliation forms the emotional heart of the book.

**2. The Return of KARAM** Five years ago, a stalker known only as **KARAM** terrorized young women in Beacon Hill — leaving notes, photographs, breaking in, occasionally attacking. The case went cold when the attacks abruptly stopped, right around the time Karisa's neighbor Karia (an investigative journalism student who may have been researching KARAM) died in an apparent suicide. Karisa left Boston shortly after.

Now that Karisa is back, KARAM — or someone using the name — has resurfaced. Threatening notes, surveillance photographs with a red X over Karisa's face, and videos of bombs planted in Aesmine's studio and David's office signal that the sisters are being deliberately targeted together.

## The Central Mystery

- **What did Karia actually discover** before she died, and was her death really a suicide?
- **Who is KARAM**, and why is he (or someone pretending to be KARAM) specifically targeting the Yeandel family?
- **Why now** — what does Karisa's return trigger?
- What is the **Yeandel family connection** to KARAM that none of the sisters can yet see?

## The Trap

The reader knows something the sisters don't: KARAM has a **handler**, and the sisters are being deliberately led down a trail of "discoveries" that feels like their own investigation but is actually a carefully constructed trap. Every clue they find, every step toward the truth, is exactly where they're being steered — and the endgame is meant to destroy them.

## Themes

Grief and homecoming, the weight of sisterhood, misogynistic violence hidden behind ordinary facades, the way old buildings and old families both "hold their secrets close," and the question of whether the past can ever really be outrun — or whether returning to it is the only way to survive it.

In short: it's a **domestic thriller about three sisters rebuilding their bond while being hunted by a stalker tied to a death they never fully understood.**
